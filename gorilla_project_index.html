Gorillas — Amazing Apes

Explore species, habitat, behavior, threats, and what people are doing to help gorillas survive.

Quick Facts

Gorillas are the largest living primates, mostly herbivorous, and live in family groups led by a silverback. There are two species (eastern and western) and several subspecies such as mountain gorillas and western lowland gorillas.

  • Scientific family: Hominidae
  • Diet: Primarily leaves, stems, fruit, and occasionally insects
  • Social: Live in troops led by a dominant male (silverback)
  • Lifespan: ~35–50 years in the wild

Where they live

Gorillas inhabit tropical and subtropical forests across central Africa. Mountain gorillas live at high elevations in the Virunga Mountains and Bwindi forest; western species live in lowland and swamp forests.

Threats & Conservation

Major threats include habitat loss, poaching, disease, and conflict. Conservation efforts combine protected areas, anti-poaching patrols, veterinary care, community programs, and ecotourism that supports local economies.

How you can help:

  1. Support reputable conservation orgs
  2. Reduce demand for illegal wildlife products
  3. Learn and share accurate information
